Research Experience

Research Assistant

TU Munich - PainLab

After high school graduation in July 2021, I organized a research gap year during which I worked as research assistant in the PainLab (@TU Munich) led by Prof. Dr. Ploner. Under the supervision of Dr. Bok, I assisted her study on study on the variability in subjective pain perception and its neural processing, while learning about cutting-edge research techniques in the fields of neurosciences, EEG, pain research and machine learning. Examples of responsibilities:

  • Supervisors: Ploner, M (Prof, PhD) & Bok, L (PhD)

  • recorded over 40 EEGs with active electrode set (and monitored EEGs for disruptive artifacts; evaluated data-quality)
  • implemented inference and bayesian Linear-Mixed Effect Models on simulated EEG data & performed model comparisons (language: R)
  • studied EEG-(pre)processing methods and pipelines (language: MATLAB & toolbox: fieldtrip)
  • assisted in three teaching workshops for EEG recording & analysis for Master's students (Neuroengineers @TU Munich)
  • visualized, processed & analyzed probands' questionnaires and evaluated applied tests (e.g. PSQI, PANAS, PSQ, HADS; language: Python & Excel)
Oct 2021 - Aug 2022
